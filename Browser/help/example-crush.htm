Crush (kr, v. t. [imp. & p. p. Crushed (kr; p. pr. & vb. n. Crushing.]
   [OE. cruschen, crousshen, Of. cruisir, croissir, fr. LL. cruscire,
   prob. of Ger. origin, from a derivative of the word seen in Goth.
   kruistan to gnash; akin to Sw. krysta to squeeze, Dan. kryste, Icel.
   kreysta.] 1. To press or bruise between two hard bodies; to squeeze,
   so as to destroy the natural shape or integrity of the parts, or to
   force together into a mass; as, to crush grapes.
